## Design Context

### Users
General public — anyone who watches IPTV, regardless of technical background. Users arrive wanting to watch TV quickly; the app should feel immediately familiar and trustworthy, like a product they paid for. Primary audience is Arabic-speaking, so RTL layout and Arabic copy are first-class, not an afterthought.

### Brand Personality
**Cinematic · Premium · Effortless**

The app should feel like it belongs alongside Plex and Netflix — polished enough that a first-time visitor assumes it's a commercial product. It earns trust through visual quality, not feature lists.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Both light and dark mode, user-switchable. Dark mode is the primary/default experience — deep navy-black (`#07090f`) canvas, a theater that puts content center stage. Light mode should feel equally premium: warm off-whites and cool grays (not stark white), never washed-out or flat.
- **Signature accent**: Blue → purple gradient (`#60a5fa` → `#a78bfa`) used deliberately on hero text, active states, and focal points — consistent across both themes.
- **Surfaces**: Layered cards with subtle borders — depth through value shifts in both themes. Dark: navy layers. Light: cool gray/white layers. No glassmorphism.
- **Motion**: Purposeful and smooth. Transitions feel responsive (~150–200ms), never jarring. The welcome glow animation sets the tone: alive, not static.
- **Typography**: Tight letter-spacing on headings, weight contrast between labels and values. Arabic and Latin text treated with equal care.
- **References**: Plex (information density + dark sidebar), Netflix (hero content focus, confident typography), Apple TV app (premium restraint).
- **Anti-references**: Cluttered dashboard tools, neon-heavy "gamer" aesthetics, anything that looks like a generic admin panel.

### Design Principles

1. **Content is the hero.** The video player and channel list are the product. UI chrome — header, sidebar, controls — should recede when not needed and never compete with content.

2. **Premium through restraint.** Every element earns its place. Prefer removing over adding. Whitespace is not wasted space — it signals quality.

3. **Fluid confidence.** Every interaction gets a response: hover states, smooth transitions, loading feedback. The app should never feel frozen or unpolished.

4. **Accessible to anyone.** No assumed technical knowledge. Labels, icons, and states must be unambiguous. Works well for first-time users and power users alike.

5. **RTL is native, not bolted on.** Arabic layout, copy, and reading direction are designed from the start — not patched over an LTR base. Alignment, flow, and spacing all respect RTL conventions.
